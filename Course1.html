Climate Change Basics

Course Summary

Climate change refers to long-term changes in Earthâ€™s temperature and weather patterns, mainly caused by human activities. The burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas releases greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide, which trap heat in the atmosphere and lead to global warming. Deforestation and industrial activities further increase these emissions.

As a result of climate change, the planet is experiencing rising global temperatures, melting glaciers, sea-level rise, extreme weather events, and changes in ecosystems. These impacts affect agriculture, water resources, wildlife, and human health across the world.

Understanding the basics of climate change is the first step toward addressing this global challenge. By adopting sustainable practices, using renewable energy, conserving resources, and spreading awareness, individuals and communities can play an important role in reducing climate change and protecting the environment for future generations.
